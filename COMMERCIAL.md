# Commercial Licensing

RogueOS is free for individuals, students, researchers, non-profits, and
small businesses. If your organisation earns more than **USD $1,000,000/year**
or you are embedding RogueOS in a commercial product, you need a commercial
licence.

This keeps development sustainable without being a barrier to the people
who actually build things.

---

| Who you are | Do you need a licence? |

| Non-profit / charity | No |

| Open source project | No (attribution required) |
| Small business < $1M/yr revenue | No (attribution required) |
| Company > $1M/yr revenue | **Yes** |
| Embedding in hardware / firmware | **Yes** |
| SaaS / managed service offering | **Yes** |
| OEM / white-label distribution | **Yes** |

---

## What a commercial licence includes

- Written permission to use RogueOS in commercial products and services
- Right to embed in hardware, appliances, or firmware
- Right to use internally across your organisation
- Right to offer derivative products commercially
- No obligation to open-source your modifications
- Attribution requirement relaxed on request (enterprise tier)
- Direct email support for integration questions
- Your name/org in the commercial users list (optional)

---

## Pricing philosophy

The goal is a small, fair contribution to continued development — not a
tax on success. Pricing is based on use case and company size:

| Tier | Who | What to expect |
|------|-----|----------------|
| Indie / Studio | < $5M/yr, single product | One-time or low annual fee |
| Growth | $5M–$50M/yr | Annual licence |
| Enterprise | $50M+/yr or OEM | Custom agreement |

These are starting points. Reach out and we'll find something reasonable.
Early adopters get better rates.

## FAQ

**Can I evaluate RogueOS before buying a licence?**
Yes. You may build, run, and test RogueOS internally for up to 90 days
for evaluation purposes without a licence, even as an enterprise. After
that, either purchase a licence or stop using it commercially.

**We're a startup, we have no revenue yet. Do we need a licence?**
Not yet. Once your annual revenue crosses $1M, you have 90 days to sort it
out. Reach out early if you want to lock in a good rate while you're small.

**We want to fork and build our own OS on top. Is that allowed?**
Under a commercial licence, yes. Under the free tier (open source), yes
provided you publish under an OSI-approved licence and include attribution.
You cannot take the code, close it, and sell it without a licence.

**What if we use only a small part of the codebase?**
The licence applies to the Software as a whole. Using a small part of it
in a commercial product still requires a commercial licence if you are an
Enterprise.

**Can I use RogueOS for pentesting client systems?**
Yes. Security research and authorised penetration testing are explicitly
free under this licence, regardless of whether you charge clients for
your services.
